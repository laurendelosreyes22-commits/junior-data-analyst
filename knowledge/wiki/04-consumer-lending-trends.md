# Consumer Lending Trends (2025–2026)

## The Macro Environment

Consumer lending in 2025–2026 is being shaped by a specific combination of forces: persistent inflationary pressure, an elevated interest rate environment that is only slowly easing, and consumers increasingly turning to credit to manage the gap between income and rising costs. According to PNC's 2026 consumer lending outlook (`10-consumer-lending-in-2026-trends-takeaways-and-the-.md`), "credit balances are climbing, financial behaviors are changing, and while interest rates are easing, they remain elevated compared to pre-pandemic levels."

The Federal Reserve Bank of New York reported that credit card balances reached $1.23 trillion in Q3 2025 — a record level. Meanwhile, FRED data as of April 2026 shows consumer revolving credit (credit cards and other revolving plans at commercial banks) at approximately $1.085 trillion, down slightly from the $1.09 trillion peak in early April but still reflecting sustained high utilization (`14-consumer-loans-credit-cards-and-other-revolving-pl.md`).

Consumer prices are approximately 26% higher than pre-pandemic levels as of early 2026, even as the inflation rate itself cools. This gap between nominal income growth and cumulative price increases is the primary driver of credit demand — consumers are borrowing to maintain living standards they can no longer fully afford from income alone.

## Personal Loan Demand: Record Highs

Personal loan demand is at a historic high. Experian data shows that 38% of U.S. consumers — roughly 1 in 3 adults — currently have a personal loan on their credit report, a share that has risen every year since 2017 (`13-personal-loan-use-reaches-new-high-rate-cuts-may-d.md`). Total personal loan balances (secured and unsecured combined) reached $597.6 billion in 2025, up 7.6% from 2024. Unsecured personal loan balances alone hit $207.1 billion, up 7.4%.

Origination volume is accelerating even faster. TransUnion reported that unsecured personal loan originations hit a record 7.2 million in Q3 2025 — the second consecutive quarter of setting new records (`14-consumer-credit-boomed-in-2025-why-2026-shows-few-.md`). Subprime borrowers drove particularly strong growth: originations among subprime consumers rose 32.5% year-over-year in that quarter, compared to 21.5% for near-prime and super-prime segments. This subprime surge is significant for EPCVIP and the lead gen industry, because subprime consumers are more likely to search online for loans (they cannot simply walk into a bank) and more likely to convert through lead gen channels.

Yahoo Finance reported a 24% increase in new personal loans in Q3 2025 year-over-year, driven by borrowers with the lowest credit scores (`12-americans-turned-to-new-credit-cards-and-personal-.md`). Michele Raneri of TransUnion noted: "People are turning to credit as we continue to see stubbornly high inflation."

Looking ahead, Experian's survey found that 42% of consumers said they are more likely to use a personal loan in 2026 due to current economic conditions — both because of lower expected borrowing rates and in anticipation of needing funds (`13-personal-loan-use-reaches-new-high-rate-cuts-may-d.md`). Personal loan hard inquiries rose 16% in 2025 versus the prior year, a leading indicator of continued demand.

## Credit Card Trends

Credit card originations also surged, rising 11.7% year-over-year in Q3 2025 — the fourth consecutive quarterly increase and the strongest annual growth rate in three years per TransUnion (`14-consumer-credit-boomed-in-2025-why-2026-shows-few-.md`). JPMorgan Chase alone added 10.4 million new card accounts in 2025.

The credit card market is evolving in parallel: higher annual fees are being offset by enhanced rewards and stronger digital security features. PNC's analysis notes an "evolving credit card landscape" where "new types of cards are emerging with customized rewards, advanced digital features, and stronger security" (`10-consumer-lending-in-2026-trends-takeaways-and-the-.md`). The average credit card APR stands at approximately 22.3%, which is high enough to motivate many cardholders to seek personal loans for debt consolidation.

A troubling signal: 12.7% of credit card balances were 90 days or more overdue in Q4 2025, the highest delinquency level since 2011. This points to financial stress among a meaningful segment of borrowers — exactly the population most likely to search for debt consolidation loans online.

## Interest Rate Environment and Its Effect on Demand

The Federal Reserve's rate policy has had a direct impact on lending demand. When the Fed cut rates, personal loan inquiries and originations accelerated — Experian's EVP Rakesh Patel noted: "Rate cuts have been a powerful near-term catalyst — they make refinancing materially more attractive and help convert consumer interest in the category into actually acquiring the loan" (`13-personal-loan-use-reaches-new-high-rate-cuts-may-d.md`).

The Fed is expected to continue lowering rates through 2026, which TransUnion forecasts will further fuel personal loan and credit card origination growth. Unsecured personal loan originations are projected to grow 5.7% in 2026, while credit card originations are projected to grow 2% after near-record growth of 9% in 2025 (`14-consumer-credit-boomed-in-2025-why-2026-shows-few-.md`).

## Geographic Concentration of Demand

Geographic demand patterns are critical for lead generation targeting. Experian data reveals clear regional stratification in personal loan uptake (`13-personal-loan-use-reaches-new-high-rate-cuts-may-d.md`):

**Highest penetration (Southern and Central states):**
- Mississippi: 53.5% of consumers have a personal loan
- Louisiana: 48.4%
- Oklahoma: 47.4%
- Texas: 46.2%
- Alabama: 50.1%
- Tennessee: 45.6%

**Highest-demand metros:**
- McAllen, TX: 57.0%
- El Paso, TX: 55.2%
- Houston: 42.8%
- Dallas: 41.3%
- Atlanta: 39.0%

**Lowest penetration (Northeastern states):**
- Massachusetts: 27.1%
- New York: 28.3% (metro: 24.4%)
- Connecticut: 28.3%
- New Jersey: 27.4%

Florida saw the largest year-over-year increase in personal loan penetration (+2.0 percentage points) — a signal that warrants attention for geographic PPC targeting.

## Implications for Lead Generation Companies

These trends create a favorable demand environment for companies like EPCVIP. Specifically:

1. **Volume growth:** Rising personal loan originations mean more consumers entering the search funnel — more potential leads for EPCVIP's publisher network to capture.

2. **Subprime surge:** The strongest growth segment is subprime borrowers who cannot easily access traditional bank channels. This is the population most reliant on online lead generation to find willing lenders.

3. **Debt consolidation driver:** With credit card APRs at 22.3% and balances at record levels, debt consolidation personal loans are in high demand. "Debt consolidation" is a core keyword category for EPCVIP's advertisers.

4. **Geographic targeting:** The Southern state concentration of demand (Texas, Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama) aligns with where EPCVIP's publisher network should prioritize lead generation traffic. Google Trends data tracking regional search interest in "personal loans" and "cash advance" can serve as real-time signals for budget allocation across geographies.

5. **Rate environment opportunity:** As the Fed continues to lower rates in 2026, personal loan search activity is expected to rise further. A PPC analyst monitoring Google Trends can identify the moment demand accelerates and shift budget toward personal loan keywords accordingly.

The CFPB's Consumer Credit Trends dashboard (`11-consumer-credit-trends.md`) provides an ongoing institutional source for monitoring these dynamics, tracking originations, inquiries, and delinquencies across credit cards, mortgages, auto loans, and student loans on a monthly basis.
