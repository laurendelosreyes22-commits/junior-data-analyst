## **Keywords Are the Building Blocks of Accounting PPC Campaigns**

Just like well-built homes rest on a solid foundation, keywords are foundational to successful accounting PPC campaigns. If you’re new to keywords and PPC keyword strategy for accountants, here’s the CliffsNotes explanation.

Keywords are simply words or phrases that people use when searching for accounting services online. If people are searching for someone to help them with their taxes, for example, they may use phrases like “tax preparation near me,” “tax preparation services,” or “tax services near me.”

So, keywords represent a strategy—the identification of key words and phrases (hence the name _keywords_) that potential clients popularly use in searches and using them as part of your PPC keyword strategy. As [QuickBooks](https://quickbooks.intuit.com/ca/resources/marketing/what-is-pay-per-click/) writes, “The right keywords make all the difference when it comes to pay-per-click advertisements. The closer your keywords match the search inquiries of your target audiences, the higher the chances are of having your ad rank at the top of their search results.”

## **Keywords Fall into Three Categories**

**Head terms** are broad, one- or two-word keywords with high search volume and competition. A few examples:

- “Accounting,” a broad term frequently searched by individuals seeking information, services, or education related to accounting.
- “Tax Preparation,” a common keyword around tax season, widely used by both individuals and businesses looking for professional tax services.
- “CPA,” popular among those seeking the services of certified accountants or information about the CPA exam and certification process.

**Medium-tail keywords** are more specific than head terms. They usually consist of two to three words and have moderate search volume and competition. A few examples:

- “Small business accounting,” often used by small business owners looking for specialized accounting services tailored to their specific needs.
- “Online tax filing,” popular among individuals and businesses seeking convenient, digital solutions for filing taxes.
- “Bookkeeping services near me,” frequently searched by those in need of local bookkeeping support.

**Long-tail Keywords** are highly specific phrases, often consisting of four or more words. A few examples:

- “Affordable accounting services for small businesses,” searched by small business owners looking for cost-effective accounting solutions.
- “Certified tax accountant near me,” used by people seeking local, certified professionals.
- “Top-rated accounting firms for nonprofits in my area,” searched by nonprofit organizations looking for accounting firms with experience in nonprofit financial management.

PPC keyword strategy for accountants involves researching and considering what categories of keyword match the objectives of your PPC campaign.

## **PPC Keyword Research Considerations**

### **Search Intent**

When potential clients Google something, there’s an intent behind their search. They have a goal. They want to find something (either answers to general financial questions or something more specific) and your accounting firm may (or likely) offer a range of services. So, PPC keyword strategy for accountants involves choosing keywords that match both search intent and your services.

Within this, there are four types of search intent to consider when planning accounting PPC campaigns:

#### **Navigational Intent**

Navigational intent keywords are essentially “how do I get there” keywords. People with navigational search intent are looking for things like direct links to a website, an address, or specific pages connected to a product or service.

#### **Informational Intent**

If a potential client searches “tax services near me,” they may not need immediate help preparing their tax return. Yet they’re certainly looking for information related to a decision, either an immediate or future one.

#### **Commercial Intent**

Potential clients searching with commercial intent are farther along in the decision stage. They know they want the help of an accounting firm. They’re actively investigating what meets their needs, which can involve any number of things, from cost to services that can help them with both immediate and long-term needs (e.g., tax preparation and retirement planning).

#### **Urgent Intent**

Urgent intent searches are “I need this now” searches. If a potential client searches for “tax services near me,” for example, the filing deadline may be approaching and they need immediate help filing their taxes.

PPC keyword strategy for accountants involves understanding the relationship between search intent and keywords to hone a keyword strategy that supports optimal PPC ad performance.

## **Search Intent Affects Quality Score and Ad Rank**

Google is in the business of matching ads and other search results according to the search intent of users. To get your ads in front of more potential clients, keywords (as the name implies) are central to accounting PPC campaigns. Yet consistency and quality as are equally important within the whole of PPC keyword strategy for accountants.

**Specifically, your ad content and landing page should match.**

If your ad content matches a user’s search intent but your landing page doesn’t offer the same level of match, Google will consider your ad content to be low quality, resulting in a low [Quality Score](https://support.google.com/google-ads/answer/6167118?hl=en) and rank in search results (plus higher cost per click) because Quality Score links to [Ad Rank](https://support.google.com/google-ads/answer/1752122?hl=en), which determines your ad’s position in search results. If your ad content and landing page both offer the same level of match to a user’s search intent, Google will consider your ad content to be high quality, resulting in higher Quality Score and higher rank in search results.

## **Choosing Keywords Involves Search Volume**

Search volume means how often people use a keyword when searching for something online. So, using high-volume keywords relevant to your accounting PPC campaigns can significantly increase visibility for your PPC ads, and keyword research tools like [Semrush](https://www.semrush.com/) can help you find them.

### **Choosing keywords based on search volume represents a balancing act.**

Choosing high-volume keywords means it may be more difficult for your firm to rank for them in searches. Low-volume keywords in accounting PPC campaigns mean fewer competitors are using them. So, you stand a better chance of ranking for them. Overall, the higher the difficulty percentage of keywords, the tougher it is to rank for them.

To help you navigate the keyword balancing act, Semrush’s Keyword Magic Tool categorizes keywords into six levels of [keyword difficulty](https://www.semrush.com/kb/1158-what-is-kd) (how hard it would be to rank for them):

- **Very Easy**, the best opportunities to rank with the least amount of effort.
- **Easy Keywords**, which have some competition but for which ranking is still achievable.
- **Possible**, requiring (per [Semrush](https://www.semrush.com/kb/1158-what-is-kd)) “quality, well-structured content that is properly optimized for your target keywords.”
- **Difficult**, requiring “ [quality backlinks](https://www.lifelearn.com/2023/04/29/veterinary-seo-checklist/) along with producing well-structured and optimized content.”
- **Hard Keywords**, requiring effort, including quality backlinks to support well-optimized and unique content.
- **Very Hard** **Keywords**, requiring significant effort.

## **The Relationship Between Search Volume and Buying Intent**

Higher keyword search volumes don’t always translate to high buying intent. The keyword “accountant,” for example, is a high-volume keyword. Yet it’s a broad word representing people at various stages of the buying cycle, from those ready to hire an accountant to others simply researching accounting services.

Buying intent with potential clients is often higher with lower-volume keywords. Someone searching “affordable tax accounting services for small businesses,” for example, has a clear and specific need that’s likely farther along in the decision-making process. Such long-tail keywords may have lower search volumes, but they typically attract better-quality leads.

Understanding the relationship between search volume and buying intent is a key part of PPC keyword strategy for accountants and optimizing accounting PPC campaigns for success.

## **Cost Per Click (CPC) Is Linked to Keywords**

As a critical metric in accounting PPC campaigns, cost per click (CPC) refers to the amount you pay each time a potential client clicks on an ad. So, it’s essential to balance CPC with your ad campaign goals and budget allocation. A higher CPC might indicate more competitive keywords, which can drive clicks but at a higher cost. Inversely, lower CPC may allow for more clicks but may not always attract the most relevant audience. The challenge in accounting PPC campaigns involves balancing these factors to meet your campaign objectives while keeping CPC at a moderate level.

### **Five CPC Tips to Optimize for Higher ROI**

- **Target high-intent keywords.** Focus on long-tail keywords with a balance of lower CPCs and high buying intent.
- **Use negative keywords.** Exclude keywords that are irrelevant or have low conversion rates to prevent wasted clicks, which can drain your budget without contributing to ROI.
- **Adjust bids based on performance.** Regularly review keyword performance and adjust your bids. Increase bids for high-performing keywords that drive conversions and decrease or pause bids for underperforming ones.
- **Geo-targeting**. Focus your ads on specific geographic regions where your target audience is most likely to convert.
- **Monitor competitors**: Keep an eye on CPC trends to stay competitive without overspending.

## **PPC vs. SEO Keyword Research**

Though keywords are integral to both accounting PPC campaigns and [SEO to rank your website higher in search results](https://www.cpasitesolutions.com/seo-for-accountants/), there are some differences when it comes to research approaches.

- In **PPC keyword research**, the focus is on finding keywords that can generate immediate results. The primary goal is to target keywords with high conversion potential, balancing factors like CPC, competition, and relevance to ad content. PPC campaigns often use highly specific, long-tail keywords that align closely with an ad’s intent. Additionally, PPC keyword research is dynamic, requiring continuous monitoring and adjustments based on performance metrics, such as click-through rates (CTR) and conversion rates.
- In **SEO keyword research**, the goal is to identify keywords that will drive long-term, organic traffic to your website. The strategy involves finding keywords with high search volume and relevance to your website’s content, with a focus on building authority and relevance over time. Unlike PPC, where immediate visibility is bought, SEO emphasizes creating content that ranks well in search engine results pages naturally. SEO keyword research is also broader, encompassing head terms, medium-tail, and long-tail keywords to capture traffic at various stages of the buyer’s journey.

Insights gained from PPC campaigns, like high-performing keywords and conversion data, can help you make sharper SEO strategy decisions. ## **How to Do PPC Keyword Research**

### **Step 1: Brainstorm Keywords**

When brainstorming keyword ideas for your accounting PPC campaigns, think broadly about how potential clients might search for the services your firm provides, then list your services as keywords (e.g., “tax preparation,” “financial planning”).

Next, consider variations and related terms users might search for, like “online tax filing” or “affordable bookkeeping services.”

Additionally, think about user intent. Some people might search for immediate help (e.g., “tax accountant near me”), while others might be in the research phase (“how to choose an accountant for my business”). This variety ensures your keywords align with different stages of the buyer’s journey.

By brainstorming from multiple angles—services, client needs, and intent—you can build a comprehensive list of keyword ideas that cover a range of potential search queries, thereby improving the relevance and effectiveness of your accounting PPC campaigns.

### **Step 2: Conduct Keyword Research**

In PPC keyword strategy for accountants, identifying optimal keywords can involve several different methods, including:

#### **Google Suggest (or Autocomplete)**

Simply launch Google, start typing a service or query related to your accounting firm in Google’s search bar, and take note of Google’s autocomplete suggestions. Per [Google](https://support.google.com/websearch/answer/7368877?hl=en#zippy=%2Cwhere-autocomplete-predictions-come-from), “Autocomplete predictions reflect real searches that have been done on Google.”

#### **Keyword Magic Tool**

[Semrush](https://www.semrush.com/)’s Keyword Magic Tool provides a wealth of information about what keywords potential clients are searching for and their search volume. This includes filtering by broad match, phrase match, exact match, and related keywords.

### **Step 3: Analyze Competitor Keywords**

Competitor keyword analysis is essential in PPC ad campaigns. By understanding which keywords your competitors are targeting, you can identify gaps, capitalize on their successes, and avoid costly mistakes. Competitor analysis helps you uncover high-performing keywords that you might have overlooked, gauge the level of competition, and refine your strategy to improve ad positioning and ROI.

To conduct competitor keyword analysis for your accounting PPC campaigns using Semrush’s [Advertising Research tool](https://www.semrush.com/features/advertising-research/):

- **Identify competitors.** Begin by listing your main competitors in the accounting industry. These could include other local firms or larger, national accounting services.
- **Access the Advertising Research tool**. In Semrush, navigate to the Advertising Research section under the Competitive Research tab.
- **Enter a competitor’s domain**. Input the URL or a competitor’s website to analyze their paid search campaigns.
- **Analyze competitor keywords.** Review the list of keywords your competitor is bidding on. Pay attention to their top-performing keywords, CPC, search volume, and ad copy.
- **Identify opportunities.** Look for keywords that are highly relevant but underutilized in your campaigns. Also, consider bidding on keywords where your competitors are seeing success but tailor your ads to offer a unique value proposition.
- **Refine your strategy.** Use insights gained from a competitor analysis to adjust your keyword strategy and better ensure your PPC campaigns are competitive and effectively targeted.

### **Step 4: Create a Final Keyword List**

Using the [PPC Keyword Tool](https://www.semrush.com/keyword-tool/) effectively is crucial for developing a robust PPC keyword strategy for accountants.

- Start by inputting broad terms related to your accounting services. This generates a list of relevant keywords, including variations and long-tail options.
- Next, organize these keywords into categories based on relevance and intent, such as “tax preparation,” “bookkeeping services,” and “financial planning.” This categorization helps create targeted ad groups and ensures that each ad group aligns with specific keyword themes.

### **Step 5: Create Keyword Groups**

Structuring keywords effectively ensures that your ads are highly relevant to user queries and provide for better budget management and performance tracking.

Some strategies for grouping:

- **By service.** Create ad groups for specific accounting services, such as “tax preparation,” “bookkeeping,” or “financial planning.” This ensures ads are tailored to each service’s unique value propositions.
- **By search intent.** Segment keywords based on user intent. This helps create ad copy that matches the user’s stage in the buying process.
- **By client demographics.** Group keywords by target audience segments (e.g., small business accounting) to tailor ads to different client needs.

Properly organized ad groups lead to more relevant ads, higher engagement, and better ROI in your PPC campaigns.

### **Step 6: Adjust Keyword Matching and Negative Keywords**

In accounting PPC campaigns, understanding keyword matching types is essential for targeting the right audience and optimizing your ad spend.

- **Broad Match.** This match type enables your ads to appear for searches that include any variation of your keyword, including synonyms and related terms. For example, if you use “accounting services” as a broad match keyword, your ad might show for searches like “financial accounting help” or “CPA services.” Yet broad match can attract irrelevant traffic and potentially waste your budget.
- **Phrase Match**. This enables your ad to appear for searches containing the exact phrase of your keyword with additional words before or after. For instance, “tax preparation services” can trigger ads for queries like “best tax preparation services” or “affordable tax preparation services.” This offers a balance between reach and relevance, ensuring your ad is shown to users searching for closely related terms.
- **Exact Match**: This match type displays ads only when the search query exactly matches your keyword or close variations. For “accounting services,” this means ads will appear for searches like “accounting services” but not “financial services.” Exact match provides high precision and relevancy but limits exposure.

In addition to this, using negative keywords is crucial to prevent your ads from appearing for irrelevant searches. For example, adding “free” as a negative keyword prevents your ads from showing for queries like “free accounting services.” Implementing negative keywords ensures your budget is spent on high-intent traffic, which improves the efficiency of your accounting PPC campaigns.

### **Step 7: Export Your PPC Keyword Campaign**

To effectively manage your PPC ad strategy, follow these steps to export keyword lists from Semrush and import them into Google Ads using [Google Ads Editor](https://support.google.com/google-ads/editor/answer/2484521?hl=en):

- **Export Data from Semrush**:
- Log in to your Semrush account and navigate to the Keyword Overview or Keyword Magic Tool.
- Enter your target keywords and view the list.
- Select the keywords you want to export. Click the Export button, usually found at the top right of the screen.
- Choose the format for export, such as CSV or Excel, and download the file to your computer.
- **Import Keywords into Google Ads Editor**:
- Open Google Ads Editor and select the account where you want to import the keywords.
- Go to the Keywords tab on the left sidebar.
- Click Make Multiple Changes, then select Add keywords.
- Choose Import and select the CSV or Excel file you downloaded from Semrush.
- Map the columns in your file to the appropriate fields in Google Ads Editor (e.g., keyword text, match type).
- Click Import to add the keywords to your campaign.

### **Step 8: Monitor Your Campaigns and Update Your Keyword Lists**

Continuous monitoring and optimization of PPC ad campaigns are crucial for accountants to ensure their advertising efforts are effective and cost-efficient. Regular campaign monitoring helps identify areas for improvement, manage budgets effectively, and maximize ROI.

Key things to monitor:

- **Conversion rate.** This metric measures the percentage of users who complete a desired action after clicking on your ad, such as filling out a contact form. A low conversion rate indicates that while your ads are attracting clicks, they may not be compelling or relevant enough to convert visitors. Improving landing page relevance and ad copy can enhance conversion rates.
- **Cost per click (CPC)**: This measures how much you pay for each click on your ad. Monitoring CPC helps manage your budget and ensures you’re not overspending on keywords. If CPC is high, consider optimizing your bids or targeting less competitive keywords.
- **Wasted spend.** This refers to money spent on ineffective clicks or irrelevant traffic. Tracking this helps identify keywords or ad groups that are not performing well, allowing you to adjust or pause them to reduce unnecessary expenditures.
- **Quality Score**: A high Quality Score can lower CPC and improve ad position. Improve Quality Score by ensuring your ads and landing pages are closely aligned with your keywords.

#### **Optimization Tips:**

- Regularly review performance metrics and adjust bids based on keyword performance.
- Refine ad copy and landing pages to improve conversion rates.
- Use negative keywords to eliminate irrelevant traffic and reduce wasted spend.
- Test different ad variations and keyword strategies to identify what works best.

By focusing on these performance metrics and continually optimizing your accounting PPC campaigns, you can enhance ad effectiveness and achieve better results.
